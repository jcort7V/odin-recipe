Tacos al Pastor

Description

Tacos al Pastor is a classic Mexican street food dish that features marinated and spit-roasted pork, typically served in soft corn tortillas. The flavorful marinade is a combination of various spices, achiote paste, and pineapple juice, giving the meat a sweet and savory taste with a hint of smokiness. These tacos are traditionally garnished with onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime, creating a delicious and satisfying culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup achiote paste
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8-10 small corn tortillas
  • 1 cup diced pineapple
  • 1/2 cup diced onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges for serving

Steps

  1. Marinate the Pork:

    • In a bowl, combine the achiote paste, pineapple juice, ground cumin, dried oregano, smoked paprika, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper to create the marinade.
    • Add the thinly sliced pork to the marinade, ensuring it's well-coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight for best results.

  2. Cook the Pork:

    • Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat.
    • Thread the marinated pork slices onto skewers or a rotisserie if available.
    • Grill the pork for about 3-4 minutes per side or until it's cooked through and has a nice char.
    • Remove the cooked pork from the skewers and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into smaller pieces.

  3. Assemble the Tacos:

    • Warm the corn tortillas on the grill or in a dry skillet for about 30 seconds per side.
    • Place a generous portion of the grilled pork onto each tortilla.
    • Top the pork with diced pineapple, onions, and chopped cilantro.
    • Serve the tacos with lime wedges on the side for squeezing over the filling.
